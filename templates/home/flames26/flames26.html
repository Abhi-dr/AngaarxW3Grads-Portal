Frequently Asked Questions

Still thinking? Here are the answers.

Who is FLAMES 26 for?
FLAMES 26 is for college students (1st–3rd year) who are done watching tutorials and want to build something real. You don't need prior experience in most tracks — just the discipline to show up every day for 45 days.
How long is the program and what's the time commitment?
FLAMES 26 runs for 45 days during summer. You'll need to commit 1.5–2 hours daily — live sessions happen 2–3 times a week, and the rest is self-paced daily tasks and project work. Consistency is what separates those who complete it from those who don't.
Can I join multiple tracks?
We strongly recommend picking one track and going deep. Splitting focus over 45 days rarely leads to a portfolio-ready outcome. If you're unsure which track to pick, click "Know More" on each card to see the full roadmap and make an informed decision.
What do I get at the end of FLAMES 26?
You walk out with: a completed real-world project you built from scratch, a completion certificate from The Angaar Batch, a GitHub commit history that proves you showed up, and the confidence that comes from actually finishing something. Most importantly, you'll have a portfolio piece — not just notes.
Are the sessions recorded? Can I watch them later?
Yes, all live sessions are recorded and shared within 24 hours. However, we strongly encourage attending live — the energy, real-time Q&A, and peer pressure of showing up are part of what makes FLAMES work.
What if I fall behind or miss a few days?
Missing a day or two is recoverable. Missing a week is a problem. We have mentors and a WhatsApp community to help you get back on track, but FLAMES is built on the assumption that you're serious. If you're not ready to commit, it's better to wait for the next batch.
Is there any placement or job guarantee?
We don't promise placements — we help you build the skills and portfolio that make you hireable. The students from FLAMES '25 who completed the program and built real projects went ahead and cleared interviews on their own. We give you the tools; you use them.
